Thanksgiving - history

First Thanksgiving

  • totally first in 1607
  • in 1619, Thanksgiving feast was held by 38 English settlers at Berkeley Hundred
    • three years later, Berkeley Hundreds was abandoned

Harvest festival observed by the Pilgrims at Plymouth

  • was held in 1621
  • the americans were taught by Indians how to catch eel and grow corn, they also gave them food during the first winter
  • commonly taken as the beginning of Thanksgiving feast Americans have nowadays
  • the feast was held after the first successfull growing season in America
  • the feast lasted for three days
  • 50 colonists and 90 natives attended

Inperiodicall Thanksgiving feasts

  • were held from 1630 to 1860
  • were held by presidents, governers and other local authorities on:
    • ends of war
    • city feasts
    • and so on
  • sometimes there were multiple in a year, sometimes there were no for several years

Civil war

  • in 1863 (during the Civil war) president Abraham Lincoln declared the final Thursday in November as Thanksgiving day
  • from that time on, Thanksgiving was every year held on that day

Post-Civil War era

  • Thanksgiving traditions varried from region to region
  • for instance, these traditions were:
    • raffles with turkeys as the main prizes
    • shooting matches, usually with the turkeys that were won earlier as targets
    • church services, usually with no attendance of turkeys
    • opulent dinner with main food - turkey (they were already shot, so what else could they do with them) and pumpkin pie
  • in other regions, these traditions were football matches or parades

Later times

  • later, there were some legal changes with Thanksgiving date:
    • during the Depresion, Thanksgiving was moved to next-to-last Thursday in November to help merchants sell Christmass goodies
    • in 1942 the Thanksgiving was moved to the fourth Thursday in November
